# About

I am a dentist building evidence-bound human-AI review workflows.

My work focuses on how clinical information is packaged, reviewed, challenged, revised, and converted into human review support without hiding the evidence trail. The goal is not to replace clinical judgment. The goal is to make AI-assisted review inspectable enough for clinical, product, informatics, and evaluation teams to understand the workflow boundaries.

## Working Thesis

Clinical AI systems become more useful when the review process makes four things explicit:

- What evidence is available
- What claims are made from that evidence
- What the reviewer accepted, rejected, or revised
- Where the workflow should stop because the evidence is insufficient

## Portfolio Signal

## Safety Boundary

This portfolio uses synthetic or de-identified research material only. It is not for diagnosis, treatment recommendation, prognosis, second opinions, medical device use, clinical deployment, or patient-facing medical advice.
